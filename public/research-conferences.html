Getting Involved in Research and Conferences

Learn how to kickstart your research journey, understand computer science conferences, and make the most out of your experience!

Getting Started with Research

  1. Identify your area of interest. Explore topics in AI, cybersecurity, data science, etc.
  2. Connect with faculty members or research labs at Hunter College.
  3. Participate in undergraduate research programs such as REUs (Research Experiences for Undergraduates).
  4. Build a strong foundation in your chosen area by completing relevant courses.
  5. Document your findings and share your work through blogs or presentations.

Types of Computer Science Conferences

  • Academic Conferences: SIGCOMM, ICML, CVPR
  • Industry-focused Events: AWS re:Invent, Google I/O
  • Hackathons: Major League Hacking (MLH) events
  • Workshops: Specialized gatherings on specific technologies or methods

Presenting at a Conference

  1. Submit your research paper or poster by the conference deadline.
  2. Prepare a clear and engaging presentation or poster design.
  3. Practice your presentation and anticipate questions.
  4. Network with other attendees and exchange ideas.
  • Hunter College has their own STEM conference every year. For 2025, the conference is on April 30th, while the abstract submission is February 10th and poster submission is March 30th.

Making the Most Out of a Conference

  • Attend keynote sessions and workshops.
  • Network with industry professionals and researchers.
  • Engage with sponsors and explore opportunities.
  • Follow up with contacts post-conference to build connections.
